Chicken Cacciatore

Description

A classic second course of popular cuisine, rich and tasty and perfect for Sunday lunch with the family.

The best way to enjoy this second course is to accompany it with some nice slices of homemade bread, maybe toasted, for a mouth-watering “scarpetta”. If we want, however, we can also combine it with a side dish of vegetables, such as a tasty caponata, grilled vegetables and classic baked potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1.2 kg chicken pieces
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 stick of celery
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 2 sprigs of rosemary
  • 100 ml red wine
  • 400 g peeled tomatoes
  • 100 g black olives
  • extra virgin olive oil
  • pepper
  • fine salt

Steps

  1. In a large pan, add a drizzle of oil and roughly chop the carrot, onion and celery directly in the pan.
  2. Light the fire and after a few minutes add the rosemary and the whole garlic clove, and let it brown.
  3. When the soffritto has wilted, place the chicken pieces in the pan, skin side down, and brown them well.
  4. Turn it from time to time to brown it on all sides.
  5. Salt and pepper the meat and mix it, then blend with the wine.
  6. Once the wine has evaporated, add the peeled tomatoes, chopping them, and a little water.
  7. Let it cook with the lid on, over low heat, for about 40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. When the cooking is almost finished (about 5 minutes) adjust the salt if necessary and then add the black olives and finish cooking.
  9. Our chicken cacciatore is ready to be served nice and hot and steaming! Enjoy your meal!
